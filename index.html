9 Most Devastating Earthquakes

Magnitude & Epicenter Depth Date Location Number of Fatalities Summary of Damages
9.5 Mw at 33 km May 22, 1960 Bio-Bio, Chile 1,655 people killed, 3,000 injured Left 2 million people homeless and caused an estimated $550 million in damages (More than $4.8 billion adjusted for 2020 inflation). It caused a tsunami that spread across the Pacific Ocean and affected the coasts of New Zealand, Japan, and the Philippines. In the coastal town of Hilo, Hawaii, a tsunami killed 61 people.
9.2 Mw at 25 km Mar 27, 1964 Southern Alaska 115 people killed, 40-50 severe injuries The earthquake and tsunamis caused an estimated $311 million in damages ($2.3 billion today). The U.S Army of Corps of Engineers spent $110 million dollars repairing infrastructure, rebuilding communities, and clearing debris.
9.1 Mw at 50 km Dec 26, 2004 Off the West Coast of Northern Sumatra 227,898 people killed, 1.7 million displaced More than 80,000 houses sustained major damage or collapsed completely. Total damages was estimated at roughly $13 billion.
9.0 Mw at 30 km Nov 5, 1952 Off the East Coast of Kamchatka Peninsula, Russia 10,000-15,000 people killed The coasts of Peru, Chili, Hawaii, and New Zealand all felt impacts from the waves. The most impacted was Hawaii having suffered an estimated $17 million in damages to shorelines and infrastructure.
8.8 Mw at 20 km Jan 31, 1906 Near the Coast of Equador 500 people killed It destroyed 49 houses and caused about $1-$5 million dollars in damages.
8.7 Mw at 30.3 km Feb 3, 1965 Rat Islands, Aleutian Islands, Alaska No Fatalities There was an estimaed $10,000 flooding damages on Amchitka Island. Minor damage recored on both Attu and Shemya islands in the form of cracks in runways.
8.6 Mw at 32 km Aug 15, 1950 Eastern Xizang-India Border 1,526 people killed 49,700 sq km suffered great damage. many buldings collaped, along with sandblows and ground cracks that all caused large landslides in certain areas.
8.5 Mw at 33 km Nov 11, 1922 Atacama, Chile 1,000 people killed A tsunami caused devastation in coastal several coastal cities, especially in Coquimbo. Total damage was estimated to be $5-25 million (1922 dollars).
8.4 Mw at 30 km Feb 3, 1923 Near the East Coast of Kamchatka Peninsula, Russia 36 people killed The tsunami that was triggered by the earthquake caused damage as far as Hawaii.A local newspaper in Russia reported that many barns and dilapidated houses were destroyed. A lot of trees tore off by their roots along the Japan Coast.
